Unified decoder-only models consolidating speech Recognition/synthesis and speech/text continuation tasks

Abstract

In recent speech processing applications, most approaches employ encoder-decoder frameworks with separate encoders and decoders for different modalities of speech and text. In contrast, we propose a decoder-only model called VoxtLM that jointly combines four tasks: speech recognition, speech synthesis, text generation, and speech continuation. VoxtLM integrates speech and text vocabulary, where pretrained self-supervised models are employed for extracting discrete speech tokens. Compared to the single task model, we show that VoxtLM exhibits significant improvement in speech synthesis, with improved speech intelligibility (reducing character error rate from 28.9 to 7.0) and improved objective quality (from 2.68 to 3.92). Additionally, VoxtLM outperforms in speech continuation and text generation and maintains competitive performance in speech recognition tasks.
